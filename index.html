About

Hi! My name is Telmo Monteiro and I am an incoming FCT Doctoral Fellow at the portuguese Institute for Astrophysics and Space Sciences (IA/CAUP) and University of Porto (starting in September 2025), working with Dr. Nuno Santos, Dr. Eduardo Cristo and Dr. William Dethier on characterization of stellar local spectra via the Doppler Shadow technique. I recently received my M.Sc. in Astronomy and Astrophysics from University of Porto, where I worked with Dr. João Gomes da Silva, Dr. Elisa Delgado-Mena and Dr. Nuno Santos on near infrared (NIR) stellar activity indicators for exoplanet detection and characterization, using HARPS and NIRPS spectrograph observations of very cool stars. I earned my B.S. in Physics at University of Aveiro, where I worked on effective temperature determination methods of FGK stars under Dr. João Fernandes and Dr. Vítor Bonifácio. During this time, I interned three times at IA (two summer internships and one university extracurricular program), working in stellar activity indicators under Dr. João Gomes da Silva, Dr. Nuno Santos and Dr. Sérgio Sousa.

My research interest is in stellar atmosphere characterization and its impact on exoplanet detection and characterization (like the popular motto says, "know thy star, know thy planet"). This encompasses stellar fundamental properties (like effective temperature), stellar variability (activity indicators) and local spectra characterization (via Doppler Shadow, and my PhD topic). My focus is on the observational side, using spectra from spectrographs like HARPS, ESPRESSO and NIRPS to extract information on the star.

I grew up in Rio Tinto, Portugal. Outside of my working desk, I am involved in student associations and I'm passionate about gaming, anime/manga and overall geek culture. I played federated handball for 8 years and had a very short handball referee career. I am mostly a rock and metal enjoyer, but also enjoy other genres (be it pop or orchestral symphonies).
